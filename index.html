About Me

Hi, I am Robert Hsuan-Fu Hua. I am currently a Ph.D. student major in statistics. I am now studying in Florida State University, Tallahassee. Prior coming to FSU. I received my Master degree in National Chengchi University, Taipei, Taiwan. It is my pleasure to be advised by Professor Ruby Chiu-Hsing Weng. I received Bachelor degree from Department of Statistics, National Cheng-Kung University, Tainan. In NCKU, I worked as an undergraduate research assistant under Professor Sheng-Mao Chang and Professor Ray-Bing Chens' adviced.

My research interest mainly lies in Bayesian inference. I am particularly interested in Bayesian model selection and Bayesian Optimization. I am also interested in sports statistics and enjoy developing models to help build a better team and search for better game-planning strategies. My past research includes player rankings dynamic modeling under the framework of the Kalman filter and unsupervised learning based on the Gaussian Mixture Model(GMM) performed on World University Rankings. Exploring different fields in statistics and applying them to real data is my passion.
